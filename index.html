McDonald’s Patties “sixty-two percent wildebeest on average”

by Fabio Derek Oswald

OCTOBER 17, 2025 - An investigation was launched Friday morning after a customer reported a meat patty was “terribly rubbery.” Thorough testing of patties from multiple restaurants indicated the patties contained an average of sixty-two percent wildebeest meat. When confronted, CEO Chris Kempczinski stated it was a “cost-cutting measure,” and “probably edible.” McDonald’s employees were interviewed to gather further information on the subject. It was found that ninety-eight percent of employees interviewed had “no idea” that the burgers contained wildebeest. All imports and exports of wildebeest have ceased as per new orders from the FDA. Food service and safety regulations are likely to become even stricter in affected areas, with larger fines for non-compliance. McDonald’s is set to lose up to $800,000,000 in fines within the next month, and stock prices have plummeted over fifty percent.
